Custom domains let you serve your app from your own domain name instead of a `*.unkey.app` subdomain. You can use either a subdomain (for example, `api.acme.com`) or an apex domain (for example, `acme.com`). Unkey handles TLS certificate provisioning and renewal automatically.

Unkey automatically detects whether you enter an apex or subdomain and chooses the appropriate verification method.

## Add a custom domain

<Steps>
<Step title="Open domain settings">

Navigate to your project in the dashboard and click **Settings**. Scroll to the **Custom domains** section.

</Step>

<Step title="Enter your domain">

Select the environment and enter the fully qualified domain name you want to use — either a subdomain like `api.acme.com` or an apex domain like `acme.com`.

<Frame>
  <img src="/networking/custom-domain-add.png" alt="Custom domains settings showing environment and domain input" />
</Frame>

</Step>

<Step title="Add the DNS records">

Unkey generates DNS records to add at your DNS provider. The records differ depending on whether you added a subdomain or an apex domain.

<Frame>
  <img src="/networking/custom-domain-verify.png" alt="DNS records to add for domain verification showing TXT and CNAME entries" />
</Frame>

**For subdomains** (e.g., `api.acme.com`):

- A **TXT** record proves ownership of the domain.
- A **CNAME** record routes traffic to your deployment. Each domain receives a unique CNAME target.

**For apex domains** (e.g., `acme.com`):

- A **TXT** record proves ownership of the domain.
- Traffic routing uses a **CNAME flattening** or **ALIAS** record (depending on your DNS provider) instead of a standard CNAME, since the DNS specification does not allow CNAME records at the zone apex.

Add the records at your DNS provider. Unkey checks for them automatically and verifies within minutes once the records propagate.

</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Both DNS records must be verified within 24 hours. If verification doesn't complete in time, the domain enters a failed state. Remove the domain and add it again to restart the process.
</Warning>

## Certificate provisioning

After DNS verification succeeds, Unkey provisions a TLS certificate from Let's Encrypt using an ACME HTTP-01 challenge. Frontline serves the challenge token automatically during this process.

Certificates renew before expiration without any action from you.

<Note>
  If Let's Encrypt rate limits are reached, certificate issuance retries automatically with backoff. This can add up to two hours of delay in rare cases.
</Note>

## DNS provider examples

### Subdomain setup

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Cloudflare">
1. Open your domain in the Cloudflare dashboard.
2. Click **DNS** in the sidebar.
3. Click **Add record**.
4. Add the TXT record with name `_unkey.{your-subdomain}` and the verification value.
5. Add the CNAME record with name `{your-subdomain}` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
6. Set the CNAME proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud) so Unkey can terminate TLS directly.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Route53">
1. Open your hosted zone in the Route53 console.
2. Click **Create record**.
3. Add the TXT record with name `_unkey.{your-subdomain}` and the verification value wrapped in quotes.
4. Add the CNAME record with name `{your-subdomain}` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Vercel">
1. Open your domain in the Vercel dashboard under **Settings** > **Domains**.
2. Click **Manage** next to your domain.
3. Navigate to the **DNS Records** tab.
4. Add the TXT record with name `_unkey.{your-subdomain}` and the verification value.
5. Add the CNAME record with name `{your-subdomain}` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
</Tab>

</Tabs>

### Apex domain setup

Apex domains cannot use standard CNAME records. Use CNAME flattening or ALIAS records instead, depending on your provider.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Cloudflare">
Cloudflare supports CNAME flattening at the apex automatically.

1. Open your domain in the Cloudflare dashboard.
2. Click **DNS** in the sidebar.
3. Click **Add record**.
4. Add the TXT record with name `_unkey` and the verification value.
5. Add a CNAME record with name `@` and the target from your Unkey dashboard. Cloudflare automatically flattens this to an A record at the apex.
6. Set the CNAME proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud) so Unkey can terminate TLS directly.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Route53">
Route53 supports ALIAS records at the apex.

1. Open your hosted zone in the Route53 console.
2. Click **Create record**.
3. Add the TXT record with name `_unkey` and the verification value wrapped in quotes.
4. Add an ALIAS record with name left blank (apex) and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Vercel">
Vercel supports CNAME flattening at the apex.

1. Open your domain in the Vercel dashboard under **Settings** > **Domains**.
2. Click **Manage** next to your domain.
3. Navigate to the **DNS Records** tab.
4. Add the TXT record with name `_unkey` and the verification value.
5. Add a CNAME record with name `@` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
</Tab>

</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Domain stuck in verifying">
Confirm your DNS records have propagated. TXT records can take up to 48 hours to propagate, depending on your DNS provider. You can check propagation with:

```bash
dig TXT _unkey.api.acme.com
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Certificate not issued after verification">
Certificate provisioning starts automatically after both DNS records are verified. If the certificate isn't issued within 30 minutes, check that your CNAME proxy status is set to **DNS only** (not proxied) at your DNS provider.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Duplicate domain error">
A domain can only be added once per workspace. If you see a duplicate domain error, check whether the domain already exists in your workspace — possibly in a different project or environment. Remove the existing entry before adding it again.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Apex domain not routing traffic">
Verify that your DNS provider supports CNAME flattening or ALIAS records at the zone apex. Standard CNAME records at the apex are not allowed by the DNS specification and will either be rejected by your provider or cause unexpected behavior. Check your provider's documentation for apex domain support.

```bash
dig A acme.com
```

The response should resolve to an IP address pointing to Unkey's infrastructure.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
