Custom domains let you serve your app from your own domain name instead of a `*.unkey.app` subdomain. You can use both subdomains (for example, `api.acme.com`) and apex domains (for example, `acme.com`). Unkey handles TLS certificate provisioning and renewal automatically.

## Add a custom domain

<Steps>
<Step title="Open domain settings">

Navigate to your project in the dashboard and click **Settings**. Scroll to the **Custom domains** section.

</Step>

<Step title="Enter your domain">

Select the environment and enter the fully qualified domain name you want to use (for example, `api.acme.com` or `acme.com`).

<Frame>
  <img src="/networking/custom-domain-add.png" alt="Custom domains settings showing environment and domain input" />
</Frame>

</Step>

<Step title="Add the DNS records">

Unkey generates DNS records to add at your DNS provider. The records you need depend on whether you're adding a subdomain or an apex domain.

<Frame>
  <img src="/networking/custom-domain-verify.png" alt="DNS records to add for domain verification showing TXT and CNAME entries" />
</Frame>

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Subdomains">
For subdomains like `api.acme.com`, add a **CNAME** record pointing to the unique target shown in the dashboard. The CNAME record is sufficient for verification — the TXT record is only needed if the CNAME cannot be verified (for example, when using a DNS proxy).
</Tab>
<Tab title="Apex domains">
For apex domains like `acme.com`, CNAME records are not allowed by the DNS specification. Instead, add:

- An **ALIAS**, **ANAME**, or **flattened CNAME** record (depending on your DNS provider) pointing to the target shown in the dashboard.
- A **TXT** record at `_unkey.{your-domain}` with the verification value from the dashboard. This record is required for apex domains to prove ownership.

<Note>
  Not all DNS providers support ALIAS or ANAME records. Check your provider's documentation for the equivalent of a CNAME at the zone apex. Cloudflare supports this through CNAME flattening automatically.
</Note>
</Tab>
</Tabs>

Add the records at your DNS provider. Unkey checks for them automatically and verifies within minutes once the records propagate.

</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  DNS records must be verified within 24 hours. If verification doesn't complete in time, the domain enters a failed state. Remove the domain and add it again to restart the process.
</Warning>

## Domain ownership and contention

If the domain you're adding is already verified by another workspace, Unkey requires a **TXT** record to prove ownership — even for subdomains where CNAME verification would normally be sufficient.

Once TXT verification succeeds, the domain is transferred to your workspace automatically. The previous workspace's domain enters a failed state and stops receiving traffic.

## Certificate provisioning

After DNS verification succeeds, Unkey provisions a TLS certificate from Let's Encrypt using an ACME HTTP-01 challenge. Frontline serves the challenge token automatically during this process.

Certificates renew before expiration without any action from you.

<Note>
  If Let's Encrypt rate limits are reached, certificate issuance retries automatically with backoff. This can add up to two hours of delay in rare cases.
</Note>

## DNS provider examples

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Cloudflare">
**Subdomains:**
1. Open your domain in the Cloudflare dashboard and click **DNS**.
2. Add a CNAME record with name `{your-subdomain}` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
3. Set the proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud) so Unkey can terminate TLS directly.

**Apex domains:**
1. Add a CNAME record with name `@` and the target from your Unkey dashboard. Cloudflare automatically applies CNAME flattening for apex domains.
2. Add a TXT record with name `_unkey` and the verification value.
3. Set the CNAME proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud).
</Tab>

<Tab title="Route53">
**Subdomains:**
1. Open your hosted zone in the Route53 console and click **Create record**.
2. Add a CNAME record with name `{your-subdomain}` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.

**Apex domains:**
1. Create an ALIAS record with name `@` pointing to the target from your Unkey dashboard.
2. Add a TXT record with name `_unkey` and the verification value wrapped in quotes.
</Tab>

<Tab title="Vercel">
**Subdomains:**
1. Open your domain in the Vercel dashboard under **Settings** > **Domains**.
2. Add a CNAME record with name `{your-subdomain}` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.

**Apex domains:**
1. Add an A record or ALIAS record pointing to the target from your Unkey dashboard, depending on what Vercel supports for your domain.
2. Add a TXT record with name `_unkey` and the verification value.
</Tab>

</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Domain stuck in verifying">
Confirm your DNS records have propagated. TXT records can take up to 48 hours to propagate, depending on your DNS provider. You can check propagation with:

```bash
dig TXT _unkey.api.acme.com
```

For apex domains, verify that your ALIAS or flattened CNAME is resolving correctly:

```bash
dig A acme.com
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Apex domain not verifying">
Apex domains require a TXT record at `_unkey.{your-domain}` to prove ownership. Make sure:

- Your DNS provider supports ALIAS, ANAME, or CNAME flattening at the zone apex.
- The TXT record name is `_unkey` (without your domain appended — your provider may add it automatically).
- The TXT record value matches the verification token shown in the dashboard exactly.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Certificate not issued after verification">
Certificate provisioning starts automatically after DNS records are verified. If the certificate isn't issued within 30 minutes, check that your CNAME proxy status is set to **DNS only** (not proxied) at your DNS provider.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Domain claimed by another workspace">
If a domain was previously verified by another workspace, you need to add a TXT record at `_unkey.{your-domain}` to prove ownership. Once verified, the domain is automatically transferred to your workspace.
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
