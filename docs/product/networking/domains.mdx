Custom domains let you serve your app from your own domain name instead of a `*.unkey.app` subdomain. You can use both subdomains (like `api.acme.com`) and apex domains (like `acme.com`). Unkey handles TLS certificate provisioning and renewal automatically.

## Subdomains vs apex domains

How Unkey verifies your domain depends on whether you're adding a subdomain or an apex (root) domain.

| | Subdomain (`api.acme.com`) | Apex domain (`acme.com`) |
|---|---|---|
| **Routing record** | CNAME | ALIAS, ANAME, or flattened CNAME (varies by provider) |
| **TXT record** | Only required if CNAME can't be verified (e.g. behind a proxy) | Always required |
| **Verification** | Automatic via CNAME lookup | TXT ownership proof |

<Note>
  Apex domains can't use standard CNAME records per the DNS specification. Providers work around this with proprietary record types. See the [provider setup](#dns-provider-setup) section for details.
</Note>

## Add a custom domain

<Steps>
<Step title="Open domain settings">

Navigate to your project in the dashboard and click **Settings**. Scroll to the **Custom domains** section.

</Step>

<Step title="Enter your domain">

Select the environment and enter the fully qualified domain name you want to use.

<Frame>
  <img src="/networking/custom-domain-add.png" alt="Custom domains settings showing environment and domain input" />
</Frame>

</Step>

<Step title="Add the DNS records">

Unkey generates DNS records to add at your provider. The dashboard shows the correct record types based on whether you're adding a subdomain or apex domain.

<Frame>
  <img src="/networking/custom-domain-verify.png" alt="DNS records to add for domain verification" />
</Frame>

**For subdomains**, a CNAME record routes traffic to your deployment. The TXT record is optional unless the CNAME can't be verified directly (for example, when using Cloudflare's proxy mode).

**For apex domains**, you need both records: an ALIAS/ANAME/flattened CNAME for routing, and a TXT record at `_unkey.{your-domain}` to prove ownership. The TXT record is always required for apex domains because the routing record isn't visible as a CNAME via DNS lookup.

Add the records at your DNS provider. Unkey checks for them automatically and verifies within minutes once the records propagate.

</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  DNS records must be verified within 24 hours. If verification doesn't complete in time, the domain enters a failed state. Remove the domain and add it again to restart the process.
</Warning>

## Domain contention

If another workspace has already verified the same domain, Unkey requires a TXT record to prove ownership regardless of domain type. Once verified, the domain is automatically transferred to your workspace and revoked from the previous one.

## Certificate provisioning

After DNS verification succeeds, Unkey provisions a TLS certificate from Let's Encrypt using an ACME HTTP-01 challenge. Frontline serves the challenge token automatically during this process.

Certificates renew before expiration without any action from you.

<Note>
  If Let's Encrypt rate limits are reached, certificate issuance retries automatically with backoff. This can add up to two hours of delay in rare cases.
</Note>

## DNS provider setup

Each provider handles apex domain routing differently. Select your provider below for specific instructions.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Cloudflare">

Cloudflare uses [CNAME flattening](https://developers.cloudflare.com/dns/cname-flattening/) to support CNAME-like records at the apex. When you add a CNAME record for your root domain, Cloudflare automatically resolves it to A/AAAA records at the DNS level.

**Subdomain setup** (e.g. `api.acme.com`):

1. Open your domain in the Cloudflare dashboard.
2. Click **DNS** in the sidebar, then **Add record**.
3. Add a **CNAME** record with name `api` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.
4. Set the proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud) so Unkey can terminate TLS directly.

**Apex domain setup** (e.g. `acme.com`):

1. Open your domain in the Cloudflare dashboard.
2. Click **DNS** in the sidebar, then **Add record**.
3. Add a **CNAME** record with name `@` and the target from your Unkey dashboard. Cloudflare automatically flattens this at the apex.
4. Set the proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud).
5. Add a **TXT** record with name `_unkey` and the verification value from your Unkey dashboard.

<Info>
  Cloudflare does not have a separate ALIAS record type. Its CNAME flattening handles apex domains transparently — you create a CNAME and Cloudflare resolves it to A/AAAA records for external DNS queries.
</Info>

</Tab>

<Tab title="Route53">

Route53 supports ALIAS records natively for apex domains. ALIAS records work like CNAME records but are resolved at the DNS level and can coexist with other record types at the zone apex.

**Subdomain setup** (e.g. `api.acme.com`):

1. Open your hosted zone in the Route53 console.
2. Click **Create record**.
3. Add a **CNAME** record with name `api` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.

**Apex domain setup** (e.g. `acme.com`):

1. Open your hosted zone in the Route53 console.
2. Click **Create record**.
3. Enter the target from your Unkey dashboard as a **CNAME** record with name left blank (root). Route53 supports CNAME-like behavior at the apex when routing to supported targets.
4. Add a **TXT** record with name `_unkey` and the verification value wrapped in quotes.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Vercel DNS">

Vercel DNS supports ALIAS records for apex domains through its dashboard. When you create a CNAME record for the root domain, Vercel automatically handles it as an ALIAS.

**Subdomain setup** (e.g. `api.acme.com`):

1. Open your domain in the Vercel dashboard under **Settings** > **Domains**.
2. Click **Manage** next to your domain, then go to the **DNS Records** tab.
3. Add a **CNAME** record with name `api` and the target from your Unkey dashboard.

**Apex domain setup** (e.g. `acme.com`):

1. Open your domain in the Vercel dashboard under **Settings** > **Domains**.
2. Click **Manage** next to your domain, then go to the **DNS Records** tab.
3. Add a **CNAME** record with name left blank and the target from your Unkey dashboard. Vercel handles apex CNAME records as ALIAS records automatically.
4. Add a **TXT** record with name `_unkey` and the verification value from your Unkey dashboard.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Other providers">

If your DNS provider supports **ALIAS** or **ANAME** records, use one of those for apex domain routing. If not, check whether your provider offers CNAME flattening.

**Subdomain setup**:

1. Add a **CNAME** record with the subdomain name and the target from your Unkey dashboard.

**Apex domain setup**:

1. Add an **ALIAS** or **ANAME** record (depending on your provider's terminology) with name `@` or blank, pointing to the target from your Unkey dashboard.
2. Add a **TXT** record with name `_unkey` and the verification value from your Unkey dashboard.

<Warning>
  Not all DNS providers support ALIAS, ANAME, or CNAME flattening for apex domains. If your provider doesn't, you can either use a subdomain or switch to a provider that supports one of these record types.
</Warning>

</Tab>

</Tabs>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Domain stuck in verifying">
Confirm your DNS records have propagated. TXT records can take up to 48 hours to propagate, depending on your DNS provider. You can check propagation with:

```bash
dig TXT _unkey.acme.com
```

For apex domains, also verify your routing record is resolving correctly:

```bash
dig A acme.com
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Certificate not issued after verification">
Certificate provisioning starts automatically after DNS verification succeeds. If the certificate isn't issued within 30 minutes, check that your CNAME proxy status is set to **DNS only** (not proxied) at your DNS provider.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Apex domain not verifying">
Apex domains require a TXT record at `_unkey.{your-domain}` because the routing record (ALIAS/ANAME/flattened CNAME) isn't visible as a CNAME via standard DNS lookup. Make sure you've added both the routing record and the TXT record.

If you're using Cloudflare, ensure the proxy status is set to **DNS only** (gray cloud).
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
