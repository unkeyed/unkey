Multi-factor authentication (MFA) adds a second verification step when you sign in to the [dashboard](https://app.unkey.com). After entering your password (or completing single sign-on), you're prompted for a 6-digit code from an authenticator app on your device.

Use MFA on any Unkey account that has access to production keyspaces, deployments, or billing. It defends your workspace against credential theft, phishing, and password reuse — an attacker who steals your password still can't sign in without the device that holds your authenticator.

## What MFA protects

MFA covers dashboard sign-in. Once you've signed in with MFA, the rest of Unkey behaves the same as before:

- API calls authenticated with [root keys](/platform/root-keys/overview) are not affected. Root keys are independent credentials with their own lifecycle — protect them by scoping permissions and rotating regularly.
- [Customer Portal](/quickstart/portal) sessions are not affected. Portal users authenticate against your application, not the Unkey dashboard.

## Supported factors

Unkey supports time-based one-time passwords (TOTP) generated by any standards-compliant authenticator app, including:

- 1Password
- Authy
- Google Authenticator
- Microsoft Authenticator

## Enroll in MFA

1. Sign in to the [dashboard](https://app.unkey.com).
2. Open your user menu and select **Security**.
3. Click **Set up two-factor authentication**.
4. Scan the displayed QR code with your authenticator app, or enter the secret manually if you can't scan it.
5. Enter the 6-digit code from the app to confirm enrollment.

Once enrolled, every future sign-in prompts you for a code from the same authenticator app.

<Warning>
  Save your authenticator backup or recovery codes somewhere safe. If you lose access to the device, you'll need them to sign back in.
</Warning>

## Sign in with MFA

After entering your password, you'll see a **Two-factor authentication** screen:

1. Open your authenticator app.
2. Enter the 6-digit code shown for Unkey.
3. The dashboard signs you in and the code is consumed — codes can't be reused.

If the code is rejected, check that your device clock is set automatically — TOTP codes are time-sensitive and drift between your device and the server will invalidate codes.

## Workspace-required MFA

A workspace can require all members to enroll in MFA. When a workspace enforces MFA:

- Members who already have MFA enrolled sign in normally.
- Members who don't are sent through the enrollment flow on their next sign-in and must complete it before they can access any workspace resources.
- Invitations to the workspace go through the same enforcement — invitees enroll during the join flow.

Workspace-required MFA does not apply to root keys or portal sessions; it only governs dashboard sign-in for human members.

## Lost device recovery

If you lose access to your authenticator app:

1. Use a backup or recovery code from your authenticator app if you saved one when enrolling.
2. If you don't have a backup, contact [support@unkey.com](mailto:support@unkey.com) from the email address on your Unkey account. Support can reset the factor after verifying your identity, after which you can enroll a new device.

Another workspace admin can also remove you from the workspace and re-invite you, which lets you re-enroll from scratch.
